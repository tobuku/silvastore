Our Story
A Landmark on Ewa Beach Road
Historic Silva Store
Ewa Beach Road

From Plantation Days to Today

Silva Store has long been one of the recognizable landmarks on Ewa Beach Road. For decades it served residents long before large shopping centers arrived in Ewa Beach.

During the plantation community era, Silva Store stood alongside Beach Corner, Wong's Chevron, and Tanaka Store as one of the few commercial establishments available to local families. It was more than a place to buy groceries — it was where neighbors gathered, stories were shared, and community bonds were forged.

"Where every customer is family and every visit feels like coming home."

Today, the location continues operating as Lloyd's Silva Store (Silva's Grocery & Liquor), carrying forward a tradition of service that spans generations. The shelves may have changed, but the spirit of aloha remains the same.

Our Heritage
A Journey Through Time

Follow the story of Silva Store from the sugar plantation days to the thriving community marketplace it is today.

Early 1900s

Plantation Era

Sugar cane fields stretch across Ewa. Plantation workers and their families build a tight-knit community in rural West Oahu.

Mid-Century

Silva Store Opens

Silva Store opens its doors on Ewa Beach Road, becoming a vital resource for local families with limited access to shops.

1960s - 70s

Community Hub

Families gather daily. Kids walk from school to buy shave ice and crack seed. The store becomes the heart of the neighborhood.

1980s - 90s

Ewa Beach Grows

New developments transform the former plantation lands. Shopping centers arrive, but Silva Store endures as a beloved local institution.

2000s

Modern Ewa Beach

The area becomes one of Oahu's fastest-growing communities. Silva Store remains a landmark amidst rapid change.

Today

Lloyd's Silva Store

Now operating as Lloyd's Silva Store, the legacy continues with groceries, liquor, local products, and a growing vendor marketplace.
